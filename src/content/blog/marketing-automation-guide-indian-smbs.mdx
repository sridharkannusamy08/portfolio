# Marketing Automation for Indian SMBs: The Beginner's System

You don't need HubSpot Enterprise. You don't need a six-figure tech stack.

You need a simple, reliable system that ensures no lead goes cold, no client gets ignored, and your follow-up happens automatically — even when you're sleeping.

This is the beginner's automation system I build for my clients. No-code or low-code. Under ₹3,000/month total.

---

## Why Automation Matters More in India

The Indian business communication landscape is unique:

- **WhatsApp is the default** — Clients expect WhatsApp responses, not just emails
- **Follow-up culture is high-context** — Multiple touchpoints are expected, not pushy
- **Business hours are fluid** — Inquiries come at all hours, often from mobile

A well-built automation system handles all of this while you focus on the actual work.

---

## The 5-Step Automation Stack

### Step 1: Lead Capture → CRM

Every lead needs to land somewhere structured.

**Tools:**
- **Tally.so** (free) or Google Forms for lead capture
- **Notion CRM** (free) or **Zoho CRM** (₹1,200/month) for lead management

**How it works:**
When someone fills your contact form, their details automatically appear in your CRM with a status of "New Lead."

Set up: Tally → Zapier/Make → Notion or Zoho CRM

**Total cost: ₹0–₹500/month**

### Step 2: Instant WhatsApp Response

The single highest-impact automation for Indian businesses.

A lead who gets a WhatsApp response within 5 minutes is **21X more likely to qualify** than one contacted after 30 minutes (Harvard Business Review).

**Tool: Interakt or WATI (₹2,000–₹3,000/month)**

Set up a WhatsApp Business API message that sends automatically when a new lead is created in your CRM.

**Template:**
> "Hi [Name], this is Sridhar from BRANDZ! Thank you for reaching out. I've received your inquiry about [Service] and will personally review it and get back to you within 2 hours with some initial thoughts. In the meantime, feel free to reply here with any specific questions. 🙏"

**Total cost: ₹2,000–₹3,000/month**

### Step 3: Email Nurture Sequence

Not everyone is ready to buy immediately. A nurture sequence keeps you top of mind until they are.

**Tool: Mailchimp (free up to 500 contacts) or ConvertKit (₹1,200/month)**

**5-Email Sequence (over 7 days):**

1. **Day 1 — Welcome + Value:** Send immediately after opt-in. Include one immediately useful tip or insight.
2. **Day 2 — Problem-Agitation:** Speak to the specific problem they're facing. No pitch.
3. **Day 4 — Social Proof:** Share a relevant case study or client result.
4. **Day 6 — Education:** A guide, checklist, or framework they can use.
5. **Day 7 — Soft CTA:** "Ready to discuss how this applies to your business?" → Book a call.

**Total cost: ₹0–₹1,200/month**

### Step 4: Proposal Follow-Up System

The highest-value automation most businesses skip.

After you send a proposal, set up a 3-step follow-up sequence:

- **Day 3:** "Just checking in — any questions about the proposal?"
- **Day 7:** Share a relevant case study that addresses their likely concern
- **Day 14:** Final follow-up with a clear deadline ("Our project slots for Q2 are filling up...")

**Tool:** Set reminders in your CRM (Zoho) or use a simple Notion system.

**Critical:** 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups. Most businesses give up after 1.

### Step 5: Client Onboarding Automation

The moment someone becomes a client is critical for retention and referrals.

**Automated onboarding sequence:**
- **Immediately:** Welcome email with next steps document
- **Day 1:** Access to shared workspace (Notion, Drive, or Trello)
- **Day 3:** Short check-in message ("Everything going smoothly?")
- **Week 1:** Project kickoff call scheduled automatically via Calendly

This removes confusion, reduces back-and-forth, and makes new clients feel confident they made the right choice.

## What to Automate vs. What to Keep Human

**Automate:**
- Initial lead response (first message)
- Nurture email sequences
- Appointment reminders
- Proposal follow-up reminders
- Onboarding instructions
- Review/testimonial requests

**Keep Human:**
- Discovery calls
- Proposal presentations
- Strategy discussions
- Relationship-building conversations
- Handling objections

The goal isn't to remove the human element. It's to ensure humans are engaged at the right moments — not wasted on repetitive tasks.
